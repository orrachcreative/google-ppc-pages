What Is a Conventional Loan?

A conventional loan is a mortgage not insured or guaranteed by a government agency — offered by private lenders under Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac guidelines. It's the most common home loan type in the U.S., offering flexibility and competitive rates for qualified borrowers.

Honest Overview

Pros & Cons of Conventional Loans

We believe in full transparency. Here's what you should know before applying for a conventional loan through Afordal.

Benefits

  • Lower rates for borrowers with strong credit (720+)
  • No mortgage insurance with 20%+ down payment
  • Flexible 10–30 year fixed and ARM options
  • Not limited by government loan rules or caps
  • Faster approval — Afordal pre-approves in 24 hrs
  • Works for primary, second homes & investment properties

Drawbacks

  • Typically requires a 620+ credit score to qualify
  • Larger down payment often needed for best rates
  • Stricter debt-to-income (DTI) requirements vs. FHA
  • PMI required if down payment is below 20%
  • Not ideal for borrowers with recent negative credit events
FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before applying for a conventional loan through Afordal.

What credit score do I need for a conventional loan?
Most conventional loans require a minimum credit score of 620. However, to qualify for the best interest rates, a score of 740 or higher is ideal. Afordal's affordability search helps borrowers with scores between 620–680 find eligible programs that minimize their cash needed to close.
How much do I need for a down payment?
Conventional loans can require as little as 3% down for first-time homebuyers. Afordal's property search automatically surfaces homes that match your down payment budget and identifies eligible low-to-no down payment programs for each specific property.
What is PMI and when can I remove it?
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) protects the lender when your down payment is less than 20%. Afordal's client dashboard tracks your equity position in real time — so you know exactly when you hit 20% equity and can request PMI cancellation. At 22% it's automatically removed.
How long does the Afordal approval process take?
With Afordal's digital process, you can receive a pre-approval in as little as 24 hours without a hard credit pull. Full loan approval typically takes 14–21 days. Our synchronized home search and lending tools streamline the transaction time frame by an average of 15%, meaning most clients close faster.
Can I use a conventional loan for an investment property?
Yes — conventional loans work for primary residences, second/vacation homes, and investment properties (1–4 units). Afordal's search can filter specifically for investment properties with financing match overlays, so you see cash flow projections alongside each listing.
What sets Afordal apart from other mortgage platforms?
Unlike other mortgage platforms, Afordal combines real-time home search with live lending data and personalized affordability filtering — so you always know exactly what you can afford and never face surprises at closing. Add in a dedicated advisor and a fully digital application, and you have everything you need to go from curious to closed.
